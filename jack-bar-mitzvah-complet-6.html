Step 1 — The Vowels (Nikud)

Hebrew letters are consonants. Vowels are tiny dots and dashes written below or above letters. 5 basic sounds: A · E · I · O · U

Vav as a Vowel ו

The letter ו (Vav) can be a consonant (V sound) or carry a vowel:

וֹ
OH
Dot on top
וּ
OO
Dot inside

The Shva ְ

Two dots under a letter = a very short "eh" or almost silent. Example: בְּ = "B'" (very short). Often appears at the start of a syllable.

Shabbat — שַׁבָּת

The Jewish day of rest. Every week from Friday sunset to Saturday night (3 stars). Commemorates the 7th day of Creation when G-d rested. The most important day of the Jewish week.

When?

🌅 Starts: Friday at sunset — candles lit 18 min before.

🌟 Ends: Saturday night when 3 stars appear (Tzet HaKochavim — צֵאת הַכּוֹכָבִים).

The Shabbat Timeline

What we don't do

39 categories of forbidden work (מְלָאכָה — melacha). The idea: stop being a "creator" for one day and just be.

🔌 No electricity🚗 No driving✍️ No writing📱 No phone🔥 No cooking💡 No switching lights

📅 The Jewish Year

A Different Calendar

The Jewish calendar follows the moon 🌙 for months and the sun ☀️ for seasons — a lunisolar calendar. 12 months (sometimes 13 in a leap year!). We are currently in year 5785 — counting from the creation of the world.

📜 Torah & Bible

What is the Torah?

The Torah (תּוֹרָה) is the Five Books of Moses — the foundation of all Jewish learning and law. Given at Mount Sinai approximately 3,300 years ago. Every week one portion (parashah — פָּרָשָׁה) is read aloud in synagogue. It takes exactly one full year to read all 54 parashas.

🥩 Kashrut

What is Kashrut?

Kashrut (כַּשְׁרוּת) is the set of Jewish dietary laws. Food that meets these laws is called kosher (כָּשֵׁר — fit or proper). Kashrut covers which animals we can eat, how they must be slaughtered, which parts are forbidden, and keeping meat and milk completely separate.
